The first thing you do is search the NASA image library by entering one or more terms. Although there are a number of space related terms, searching on other terms, like places on Earth and famous people, also yields good results. The results are currently limited to about 20 per page.

After you've selected an image, you describe what you want to do with it. This is called a prompt. For example, you could say "Render this as a pastel drawing." Or you could try "Turn the astronaut into a cat." The possibilities are endless.

For this, you'll need a Stable Diffusion API key. Don't worry -- it doesn't require a credit card and you get a generous amount of free usage. You can get one by logging into Dream Studio. Dream Studio is pretty cool, but does it offer the ability to search for NASA images? No, it does not.

After the app sends the image back, you can easily resubmit it with more prompts if you wish to. Who knows where you'll end up?
